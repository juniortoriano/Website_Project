Fremont, California

Fremont is a large city that is located in the northern part of California of the San-Franciso Bay Area. It is part of the Alameda County district. The city is divided into five main areas: Centerville, Niles, Irvington, Mission San Jose, and Warm Springs.

The Districts of Fremont

The first of these main areas is called Centerville. It is the district of Fremont that makes of the center of the city and its northern parts. There are several elementary schools that are located in this district, but the main schools are its middle school, called Centerville Junior High School and Washington High School. In addition, there are the other notable pair being Thornton Junior High School and American High School

The next of these districts is the Niles district of Califronia. This is the oldest district of the city and it carries a lot of history with it. In the area, there are many older citizens and establishments. The schools of the Niles district are the same as those in the Centerville district because they are so close together. As such, the students of that district enroll in the same middle schools and highschools as those from Centerville.

Another district of Fremont is the Irvington district. This is also a fairly old district of California that is still very popular. It is located towards the southern side of the city. The notable street that runs through this district is Blacow Boulevard. The two main highschools that are located in this district are Irvington High School and John F. Kennedy High School.

Mission San Jose is another popular district of Fremont. It is located very close to the Niles district and it is also an older area of Fremont. It contains the most notable structure in the city being Mission San Jose, a Catholic foundational church that was established here by migrating priests. Also in the district is one of the most recognized community colleges in the state of California called Ohlone Community College. The highschool for this district is called Mission San Jose High School respectively.

The final district of Fremont is the Warm Springs district. This area is a very old district in California. It is very small and covers the southern most area of Fremont before entering into the city of Milpitas. The city has no formal highschool, so its students attend the highschools that are closest to them being Irvington or Mission San Jose High School. The district is very populated with shops as it has a very popular BART station located in it.
